1. What is the Model?

Your model is LightGBM (Light Gradient Boosting Machine) - a powerful machine learning algorithm that predicts automobile sales units based on historical data and market conditions.

Model Specifications:

  • Algorithm: LightGBM Regressor
  • Purpose: Predict Units Sold per month

  • Prediction Time: Less than 1 second

2. How It Works - Step by Step

Phase 1: Training (Learning from History)

The model learns from 5+ years of historical data for different car models. For each record, it has:

  • Date: When the sale occurred (January 2020 → May 2026)
  • Sales Volume: Number of units sold
  • Price: Vehicle pricing (₹1.2M, ₹1.5M, ₹1.8M)
  • Discounts: Promotional offers (2%, 5%, 8%)
  • Finance Rate: Loan interest rates (6.5%, 8%, 9%)
  • Lead Count: Number of potential customers

From this data, the model learns patterns such as:

  • ✓ "When price is high AND discount is high → Sales usually increase"
  • ✓ "When finance rates are low → More people take loans → Higher sales"
  • ✓ "Each car model has unique sales patterns"
  • ✓ "Seasonal trends affect sales differently by model"

Phase 2: Validation

After training, the model is tested on data it has never seen before (the last 3 months of historical data). This ensures the model generalizes well.

Results:
• MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error): 20.39%
• RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error): ~2.5 units
• Interpretation: Predictions are typically within ±20% of actual values

4. LightGBM Decision Making

LightGBM is an ensemble method - it combines 500 simple decision trees to make powerful predictions.

How a Single Decision Tree Works:

Is Price > ₹2,000,000? ├─ YES → "High-Income Customers" │ Is Discount > 5%? │ ├─ YES → Predict: 20 units │ └─ NO → Predict: 10 units └─ NO → "Budget-Conscious Segment" Is Finance Rate < 7%? ├─ YES → Predict: 25 units └─ NO → Predict: 15 units

Your Model Configuration:

  • n_estimators = 500: 500 decision trees work together
  • learning_rate = 0.05: Each tree contributes 5% weight (conservative)
  • max_depth = 6: Trees can be maximum 6 levels deep
  • num_leaves = 31: Maximum 31 endpoints per tree
  • subsample = 0.8: Each tree uses 80% of the data
  • colsample_bytree = 0.8: Each tree uses 80% of features
